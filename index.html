Áo Dài

The áo dài is a Vietnamese traditional clothing, now most commonly worn by women but can also be worn by men. In its current form, it is a tight-fitting silk tunic worn over trousers.In modern life Aozai is worn by Vietnamese women at festive events, in a solemn atmosphere, and it is also a uniform of schoolgirls, female students and employees of many companies in Vietnam.

Street food

Comprised of chaos and charm in equal parts, Hanoi's Old Quarter is both fun to explore and a foodie's dream, offering some of the best street food in the world. Small local street food eateries are everywhere in Hanoi, with the smoke from chargrilled pork wafting through the streets and a bubbling stockpot of fragrant pho on every corner, and not a McDonalds in sight.
